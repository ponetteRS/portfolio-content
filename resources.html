When I was figuring out my skin care routine something that really helped me was finding information from other people interested in skin care. Some of the resources that I found really helpful were communities on reddit. The reason why I found read reddit a very useful resource was because I was able to get tips and advice from people first hand such as new websites and other addditional resource hubs. Additionally since posts can reach many people I was able to find out differing opinions and tips for finding a derm. I also found a sense of community on the skin care page and I felt like I was able to have a sense of camaraderie going into the skin care process as a newbie. Something that I would advise against is taking information as a fact online running it through a fact checker like Snopes. The pages I used were nice for basic skin care like cleansers and how to find dermswhich did not cover medical concerns. Even for basic skin care, opinions vary so I try not take person's idea as a fact and make sure to always be open to new information as well as strive to get reputable sources for things like medical questions.
